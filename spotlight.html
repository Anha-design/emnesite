ALL THINGS MOVIE

Forside / Spotlight

We all go a little mad sometimes

Why do people love horror movies? Maybe it’s the adrenaline rush, the thrill of facing fears safely, or the chance to explore the unknown. Maybe it’s laughing and screaming with friends or testing how far you can push yourself. Horror is scary, exciting, and strangely satisfying all at once. And whatever you do, don’t split up.

Adrenaline and Excitement

Horror movies trigger a fight-or-flight response in the brain, releasing adrenaline and other stress hormones. This creates a thrilling, intense sensation similar to riding a roller coaster. Some people love the rush of fear because it’s exciting but safe; they know they are not actually in danger.

Catharsis

Watching horror can be a way to release pent-up emotions like stress, anxiety, or anger. Experiencing fear on screen can make people feel a sense of relief or emotional cleansing afterward, almost like purging negative feelings in a controlled environment.

Adrenaline and Excitement

Many enjoy watching horror with friends or family because shared fear can strengthen bonds. Laughing, screaming, or jumping together creates a sense of community.

Testing Personal Limits

Horror allows people to confront fear in a controlled way, which can feel empowering. They can challenge themselves to stay calm, analyze threats, or simply survive the movie emotionally.
